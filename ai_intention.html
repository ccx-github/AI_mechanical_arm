AI Intention Recognition

Questions: Why do you say your product is related to AI, and what are the benefits of this?

Answer

Our product provides a solution for hand rehabilitation through the combination of AI intention recognition and hand rehabilitation exoskeletons. AI intention recognition can identify the hand movements of individuals requiring rehabilitation training and adjust the exercises in real-time, allowing patients to undergo rehabilitation training to the fullest extent within a safe range of motion. Moreover, the actual data can be viewed by the patients themselves and transmitted to rehabilitation doctors, enhancing the effectiveness of rehabilitation training. The classic analysis method is divided into two steps: offline model training and online model recognition.


In offline model training, the first step is to collect electromyography (EMG) training data. The collected raw EMG signals are first subjected to filtering and noise reduction processing to minimize the impact of noise and other factors. Features are extracted from the EMG signals, and the collected features are normalized. Finally, the processed feature data is used to train and obtain a training model.


The online model recognition process is similar to offline model training. Real-time collected EMG data is used as input. The real-time collected EMG signals undergo signal preprocessing, feature extraction, and feature normalization before being transmitted to the offline-trained model. With accurate data, real-time hand movement intentions are calculated and obtained.


During the rehabilitation process, real-time monitoring of electromyography (EMG) and providing patients with EMG feature feedback can effectively assist them, encouraging autonomous training and thereby enhancing the effectiveness of exercises. To make the rehabilitation process more visible, boost patients' confidence in recovery, and simultaneously prevent fatigue and over-rehabilitation of hand muscles during the process.

Technical Principles

By collecting EMG data, calculating corresponding fatigue characteristics, and displaying them in real-time, the results will be utilized in the design of control software to achieve excellent human-computer interaction. Under static and dynamic conditions, muscle fatigue causes different changes in EMG signal characteristics. During repetitive work-induced muscle fatigue, EMG characteristics exhibit increased amplitude and a leftward shift in the frequency spectrum.

Feature Extraction of RMS for 8-Channel EMG using MYO Armband


The increase in EMG amplitude during muscle fatigue is due to the recruitment of more motor units to compensate for the decreased muscle force of already activated motor units in order to complete fixed movements. The leftward shift may be related to a decrease in muscle fiber conduction velocity (MFCV).
