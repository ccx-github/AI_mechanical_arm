Question: How do you ensure that the equipment is lightweight and that it will last a long time?

Answer

We use stainless steel, polyurethane, and composites. We chose polyurethane and composites because of their high strength and flexibility while being inexpensive, and we chose stainless steel because of the potential for consumers to use our products in any situation.
Our tests use thin-walled steel sheets to actuate the hand exoskeleton. When the pressure is fixed at both ends, the thin-walled steel sheets are used to actuate the hand exoskeleton. Exceeding a certain load, the thin-walled steel sheet will flex, forming a curved shape. The flexural flexibility of the thin-walled steel sheet can be changed by varying the size of the sheet, the end boundary conditions, and the type of material (stainless steel, polyurethane, composite, etc.). Modeling of thin-walled steel sheets using the deformation equation yields the optimum thickness and operating conditions for these materials.

Technical Principles

Set up the working conditions:
(1) The elastic properties of the thin-walled steel sheet are linear;
(2) The pressure on the steel sheet is in the normal range;
(3) Buckling deformation after thin-walled steel sheet.

Model

The thin-walled steel strip is simplified as a slender beam with a width of b, a thickness of h, and an undeformed length of L. In the pre-buckling state, an axial displacement δ is applied to one end of the thin-walled steel strip, generating an end load P. At this point, the thin-walled steel strip undergoes axial compression deformation without out-of-plane deformation. When the end displacement continues to increase, causing the end load to exceed the critical buckling load Pcr, the thin-walled steel strip will buckle. Conclusion

The thin-walled steel plate serves as the main driving component of the finger exoskeleton, and its structural parameters directly affect the exoskeleton's bending performance. Experimental results show:
Increased Thickness: The bending angles of the DIP (distal interphalangeal joint) and MCP (metacarpophalangeal joint) decrease, while the bending angle of the PIP (proximal interphalangeal joint) increases. This is because the MCP and DIP joints are closer to the fixed end of the steel plate, resulting in higher rigidity and requiring more thrust force to bend. The PIP joint, located in the middle, has less rigidity, so when the thrust force increases, its bending angle increases.
Increased Width: The bending angle of the PIP joint remains relatively unchanged, the bending angle of the MCP joint decreases, and the bending angle of the DIP joint increases. Increasing the width reduces the stiffness of all joints, with the DIP joint experiencing the greatest reduction. As a result, when the thrust force increases, the bending angle of the DIP joint increases, while the MCP joint's bending angle decreases due to a smaller change in stiffness.
By adjusting the thickness and width of the steel plate, the bending angles of the exoskeleton's joints can be controlled, making them closer to those of the human hand, thereby improving comfort and practicality.

Question: How to improve the rehabilitation training program through the product?

Answer

We designed the myoelectric sensing bracelet to realize safe and efficient rehabilitation training for patients' hands, which requires the collection of information about the robot's working status during rehabilitation training, including information about the angle of the finger joints, the exoskeleton driving force patients' forearm myoelectricity, and so on. With this information we can then monitor and improve the rehabilitation training program in real time.

The MYO EMG Arm Loop Sensor has 8 channels to collect information to ensure reliability, the EMG signal is collected at 200 Hz, it is easy to wear, low cost, low power consumption, and has good practicality, the collected sEMG will be transmitted to the host computer through low power Bluetooth.

Technical principle

Various movements of the hand are accomplished by the interplay of skeletal muscles, in which each muscle fiber acts as an independent motor function and structural unit. Surface electromyography (sEMG) is a non-smooth and weak signal generated by motor unit action potential train (MUAPT) superimposed on the skin of human forearm under the control of central nervous system, and its generation principle is shown in the following figure. The principle of generation is shown in the figure below. The action potential can essentially reflect the muscle activity.

The sEMG is a non-smooth and weak signal with an amplitude range of 0 ~ 1.5 mV, 60 ~ 300 uV during muscle contraction and 20 ~ 30 uV during relaxation. sEMG is useful at 20 ~ 500 Hz, and most of the spectral energy is concentrated in the range of 50 ~ 150 Hz. sEMG has variability, reproducibility, and similarity. Because it is generated 30 ~ 150 ms in advance of human movements, it can be used in the field of gesture recognition and provides a prerequisite for the development of the ai intent recognition function that we will carry out later.
