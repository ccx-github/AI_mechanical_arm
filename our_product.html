Our Product

The AI-driven Hand Rehabilitation Exoskeleton by RevoHands is an innovative solution designed to assist patients recovering from hand dysfunction, with a primary focus on stroke survivors. Unlike traditional rehabilitation devices, which often lack adaptability and precision, our product integrates cutting-edge technology to provide a personalized, adaptive, and effective recovery experience.

Core Features

Five-Finger Independent Movement

Our exoskeleton is designed with five independently controlled fingers, mimicking the natural motion of the human hand. This enables patients to regain fine motor control and execute complex movements that are often impossible with other devices. Whether it's grasping an object or performing intricate tasks, this feature helps patients achieve greater rehabilitation outcomes.

AI-Based Intent Recognition

The device features advanced AI-driven intent recognition that can identify the patient's specific motor intentions in real time. By utilizing sensors that track muscle signals and movement patterns, the system adapts the rehabilitation exercises according to the patient’s current physical state, ensuring that each session is tailored to their needs. This dynamic approach accelerates the recovery process while reducing the risk of injury or overexertion.

~

Modular and Lightweight Design

Weighing in at just 200g, the exoskeleton is both modular and lightweight, making it easy to wear for long periods of time. This design allows for adjustments to fit different hand sizes and ensures comfort, enabling patients to conduct rehabilitation exercises at home without discomfort. The portability and simplicity of the design make it a versatile tool for continuous therapy.

Real-Time Feedback and Monitoring

Equipped with multiple sensors, the exoskeleton continuously monitors key metrics like joint angles, force exerted, and motion speed. These metrics are instantly sent back to the patient’s interface, offering real-time feedback. This feedback allows users to adjust their movements and maintain proper technique, which enhances both safety and effectiveness throughout the rehabilitation process.
